# AAPL (Apple) Pre-Registration
## Date: April 2026
## Pre-committed before any pre-screen or pipeline execution

## Predicted signal: NO SIGNAL

## Five-condition plus C7 assessment

### C1 Consumption revenue: PARTIAL
App Store and services revenue (Apple One, iCloud, Apple TV+,
Apple Music) is consumption-based and growing rapidly.
Services revenue approximately 25% of total and accelerating.
However hardware (iPhone, Mac, iPad, Wearables) remains
dominant at approximately 75% of revenue and is not
consumption-billed. Partial credit only.

### C2 Embedded platform: PASS
iOS and macOS ecosystem lock-in is among the strongest
in all of technology. iMessage, FaceTime, AirDrop, Handoff,
iCloud sync, Apple Watch integration, and the App Store
create a switching cost that is functionally prohibitive
for most users. Full credit.

### C3 Frictionless consumption uplift: PARTIAL
App Store improvements and platform enhancements
automatically generate more app consumption from existing
users without active adoption decisions. Better Siri,
improved Photos ML, faster on-device processing all
generate more service usage.
However the primary revenue driver (hardware) requires
active purchase decisions -- a better iOS does not
automatically generate more iPhone revenue from existing
customers. It may accelerate upgrade cycles but this
is indirect and lumpy not frictionless.
Partial credit only.

### C4 Near-term revenue realisation: PARTIAL
Services consumption realises within the quarter.
Hardware revenue depends on iPhone upgrade cycles
which are 2-3 year decisions not continuous consumption.
Partial credit only.

### C5 Product revenue primacy: FAIL
Apple's stock trades primarily on:
- iPhone unit volumes and ASPs
- China sales exposure and geopolitical risk
- Hardware margin dynamics
- Supercycle narratives (iPhone 16, Apple Intelligence)
Services growth is valued positively but the stock's
primary valuation driver is hardware. A 1% miss on
iPhone units moves the stock more than a 10% beat
on services revenue.
Engineering investment in GitHub-observable developer
tools has no direct relationship to iPhone sales volumes.
FAIL -- stock does not trade on platform engineering.

### C6 Open-source observability: PARTIAL
Apple has a GitHub presence (apple org) with:
- Swift compiler and standard library
- Swift Package Manager
- SwiftUI and developer frameworks
- LLVM contributions
Corporate email rate appears high in these repos.
However the strategically decisive engineering --
Apple Silicon chip design, iOS kernel, Face ID,
Secure Enclave -- is entirely proprietary.
Same Type 3 C6 constraint as FSLY -- platform-adjacent
repos but core engineering invisible.
Partial credit only.

### C7 Engineering investment compounds the moat: UNCERTAIN
Apple Silicon (M-series chips) represents a genuinely
compounding technical advantage -- years ahead of Intel
and competitive with AMD in performance per watt.
However Apple Silicon engineering is entirely proprietary
and not observable via GitHub.
The GitHub-observable engineering (Swift, developer tools)
compounds the developer ecosystem moat but this does not
directly drive stock price movements which are dominated
by hardware cycle dynamics.
Uncertain -- the compounding advantage exists but is
not observable.

## Predicted signal: NO SIGNAL

Primary reasons:
1. C5 FAIL -- stock trades on hardware cycles not
   platform engineering investment
2. C6 Type 3 constraint -- core engineering (chip design,
   iOS kernel) not observable via GitHub
3. Even if signal existed in services layer it would be
   drowned out by hardware cycle noise in returns

## Confidence level: HIGH
C5 failure is definitive. Apple's stock is the world's
most widely followed hardware consumer company. No
amount of Swift compiler improvements will predict
iPhone upgrade cycles.
