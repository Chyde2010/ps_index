# FSLY (Fastly) Pre-Registration
## Date: April 2026
## Pre-committed before any pre-screen or pipeline execution

## Predicted signal: NEGATIVE

## Five-condition plus C7 assessment

### C1 Consumption revenue: PASS
CDN and edge compute billed on traffic volume and requests.
Genuine consumption model -- more traffic means more revenue
automatically from existing customer workloads.

### C2 Embedded platform: PARTIAL
CDN is embedded in customer infrastructure but switching
costs are lower than application platforms. Cloudflare,
Akamai and AWS CloudFront are credible alternatives with
manageable migration paths. Partial credit only.

### C3 Frictionless consumption uplift: PASS
Edge compute improvements automatically serve more traffic
from existing customer workloads. Better cache hit rates
and lower latency generate more requests handled without
requiring active customer adoption decisions.
Contrast with PATH (C3 fail) where new features require
customers to build new workflows.

### C4 Near-term revenue realisation: PASS
Traffic consumption bills immediately within the quarter.
No procurement cycle or adoption lag between engineering
improvement and revenue impact.

### C5 Product revenue primacy: PASS
Direct product revenue from CDN and edge compute.
No advertising or hardware revenue. Stock trades primarily
on revenue growth and consumption metrics.

### C6 Open-source observability continuity: UNCERTAIN
Fastly has a GitHub presence but the quality and corporate
email observability needs to be verified before pipeline.
Pre-screen required to confirm C6 before full pipeline.

### C7 Engineering investment compounds the moat: FAIL
Fastly competes against:
- Cloudflare: structural network scale advantage (300+ PoPs
  vs Fastly ~80), superior AI/Zero Trust product suite,
  stronger developer platform with Workers
- Akamai: decades of CDN infrastructure investment,
  dominant enterprise relationships
- AWS CloudFront: deep AWS integration, essentially
  unlimited resources

Fastly's Varnish-based edge compute architecture is
technically sophisticated but not structurally inimitable.
Cloudflare has replicated and exceeded Fastly's edge compute
capabilities with workerd/Workers. Engineering investment
at Fastly maintains competitive parity against structurally
superior incumbents -- the same C7 failure mechanism as GTLB
(GitLab vs GitHub) and DDOG (competitive API parity).

## Predicted signal direction
NEGATIVE -- same theoretical mechanism as DDOG, TWLO, GTLB.
Engineering investment signals competitive necessity and
cost pressure rather than compounding platform advantage.

## Confidence level: MEDIUM
- C7 failure is clear -- Cloudflare's structural advantages

  repos are thin or peripheral the pipeline may produce
